Research / SPARC

SPARC - SPectral ARC length is a method for quantifying the smoothness of a movement. The SPARC takes a radically different approach to the smoothness quantification problem. The most popular approach prior to the SPARC was the jerk-method, where the smoothness of a movement was quantified using the normalized squared jerk of a movement. The SPARC, on the other hand, looks at the Fourier magnitude spectrum of a movement's speed profile to quantify movement smoothness. Changes in movement smoothness are reflected as changes in the complexity of the magnitude spectrum, which is quantified by calculating the length of the magnitude spectrum curve.

We recently also provided a method for quantifying the smoothness of rhythmic movements and showed that smoothness is task-dependent and one cannot simply the smoothness of two different movements.

We are currently working on methods to quantify movement smoothness using data from inertial measurement units.
